Reflection questions

  1. What happened to the particles when the temperature increased?
  2. What happened to the particles when the temperature decreased?
  3. How were the particles different in a solid, liquid, and gas?
  4. What happened to pressure when the container became smaller?
  5. What happened to pressure when more particles were added?
  6. Why is a particle simulation useful for understanding matter?
  7. What evidence from the simulation supports your conclusion?
Teacher note: This simulation is intentionally simplified. It is not a perfect molecular dynamics model. Its purpose is to help students link visible particle behaviour with scientific explanations: heating increases energy, cooling reduces energy, gases collide with container walls, and pressure increases when particles are hotter, more numerous, or squeezed into a smaller space.
